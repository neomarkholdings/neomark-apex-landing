2026 spotlight

Michigan's 2026 Medicaid GLP-1 mandate, line by line

A concrete example of the detail in the dataset. On January 1, 2026, MDHHS limited Medicaid GLP-1 weight-loss coverage to a BMI of 40 or higher, or 35 or higher with an obesity-related comorbidity such as hypertension, dyslipidemia, or obstructive sleep apnea. We parsed the published criteria directly and confirmed 35 distinct GLP-1 coverage restrictions at the formulary-line level. These are the graded rows your prior-authorization team works from, not a summary of a press release.

Diabetes path: Type 2 only

Ozempic · Rybelsus

Covered for Type 2 diabetes only and excluded for weight loss. A weight-loss prior authorization on these products is denied by rule, not by reviewer discretion.

Obesity path: per-state BMI

Wegovy · Zepbound

Covered for chronic weight management subject to the per-state BMI threshold, prior authorization, and step therapy. Michigan's 2026 floor is BMI ≥ 40, or ≥ 35 with a comorbidity.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Michigan Medicaid GLP-1 mandate for 2026?

Effective January 1, 2026, MDHHS restricts Medicaid GLP-1 weight-loss coverage to members with a BMI of 40 or higher, or 35 or higher with an obesity-related comorbidity. Neomark has confirmed 35 distinct MDHHS GLP-1 coverage restrictions at the formulary-line level.

Does Michigan Medicaid cover Ozempic and Wegovy in 2026?

Coverage splits by indication. Ozempic and Rybelsus are covered on the diabetes (Type 2) path only and are excluded for weight loss. Wegovy and Zepbound follow the per-state obesity path and require the BMI thresholds and prior authorization defined by each state's Medicaid program.

What BMI is required for Medicaid GLP-1 prior authorization?

Under the 2026 Michigan mandate, the obesity path requires a BMI of 40 or higher, or 35 or higher with a qualifying comorbidity. Thresholds and step-therapy depth vary by state, which is why NDC-level, state-by-state data matters.
